About the Author

Sharon Aicler is a software architect with more than two decades of experience building and operating large-scale distributed systems.

Working across networking, security, and platform infrastructure, he observed that most technology initiatives fail economically before they fail technically. Costs rise, delivery slows, and teams burn out not because of talent or effort, but because architectural intent is left implicit.

This book is written from that perspective. It connects architecture to cost, risk, and organizational drag, and presents Layer 8 as a structural response: making intent explicit so systems scale without coordination overhead, heroics, or compounding complexity.

The focus is not optimization. It is removing costs that should not exist.
